Equality in Algebra?

An equality in algebra can be defined as a mathematical statement in which two expressions are joined together with an "equal to (=)" sign, and both expressions are set equal to each other. An equality usually consist of a variable, coefficients, and constants. The value of the variable that satisfies the equations to be true in known as a solution for the equality. There are several types of equality in algebra such as, linear equations, quadratic equations, and polynomial.

Part 1

Linear Equations

A linear equation is an equation in which the highest power of the variable is always 1. It is also known as one-degree equation. A linear equation is represented by a straight ine graph in coordinate plane. General form of a linear equation with two variable is Ax + By = C, where A, B, and C are real numbers where both coefficients of the variables must not equal to zero simultaneously.

For Example:

2x + 3y = 4

2 and 3 are coefficients, 4 is a constant, y = 1, and x = 1/2 are one of the solution that satisfy the equation.

y = 4x + 7

The equation above can be rewritten as y - 4x = 7. Therefore, 1 and 4 are coefficients, 7 is a constant, y = 11, and x = 1 are one of the solution for the equation.

2x = -3

This equation is also a linear equation with one variable. 2 is a coefficient for x variable, -3 is a constant, and x = -3/2 is the solution for the corresponding equation.

How to solve a linear equation?

Linear Equation with One Variable

Solving a one variable linear equation is easier because we just need to rewrite the equation and separate the variable on the variable on the one side, and the constant on the other side.

Example:

x + 2 = 4

x = 2

2 is subtracted from both side

2x + 3 = 7

2x = 4

x = 2

3 is subtracted from both side and both side is divided by 2

2x = 3

x = -3/2

The both side of the equation is divided by 3

How to Solve a Linear Equation?

Linear Equation with Two Variables

There are some methods to solve linear equation with two variables, such as Subtitution Methods, Elimination Methods, and Graphical Methods.

Subtitution Methods

In substitution method, the value of one variable from one equation is substituted in the other equation. In this way, a pair of the linear equation gets transformed into one linear equation with only one variable.


Elimination Methods

This method involves adding or subtracting the equations to cancel out one variable, allowing you to solve for the other. Once one variable is found, you substitute it back into one of the original equations to find the second variable.


Graphical Methods

A graphical method involves plotting each equation on a coordinate plane and finding the point(s) where the graphs intersect. This intersection point represents the solution to the system of equations.
